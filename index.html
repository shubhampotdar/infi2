ABOUT US

Infi-Alpha Hyperloop is a team of undergraduates based in SRM institute of science and technology, working on the research, development and design of Hyperloop System. Every great advancement was once just a dream in a visionary’s head. In the coming years, Hyperloop pertains itself to the pool of technologies attempting to revolutionize global lifestyle. And our team aims to implement the first Hyperloop system in India.

TEAM AND DOMAINS

Infi Alpha Hyperloop is the only research based competition oriented team operating in SRM University with goals set for long term and short term. The team comprises of 20 core members divided according to their fields of expertise into 5 broad domains, namely:


Structured Design and Analysis
Shell and Chassis: The shell of the pod is in a form of a hybrid secant- tangent ogive. The entire pod is made up of carbon fiber; IM10. The drag coefficient is maintained at 0.27, thus having no effect on the speed of the pod. The pod overall has an aerodynamic shape.

Stability Mechanism: The stability mechanism of the system prevents the slippage of the system in any direction and ensures the desired motion. It consists of 4 wheels (2 pairs), made of poly urethane and providing yaw arrest for the system.

Drive Train: The drive train system comprises of a motor connected to a gear. The driver and the driven gears are meshed by a timed belt and rotate at a ratio of approximately 1:3. A coupler, which is a flexible shaft, is attached to the driven gear on one end and the wheel on the other, ensuring the movement of the later.
Propulsion and Braking System
Propulsion system: The propulsion system provides speed for the forward movement of the pods. It works on a contact based tractive effort reaching a speed of 120 m/s, accelerating at 1.22g. It involves a synchronized motion of all the wheels giving rise to an “All Wheel” drive.

Braking system: The braking system of the Hyperloop system ought to be extremely effective and jerk-less, and for the same the braking system is divided into two phases. First; non-contact braking, which reduces the speed of the system from 120 m/s to 40 m/s. Second, contact braking which follows the non-contact braking brings the system to an ultimate halt within 2.4 seconds. The entire braking system is proposed to have a span of 7 seconds.
Electronics and Embedded Systems
Embedded System and Battery: Data Acquisition System gathers information from all the sensors and acts as a mediator between the sensor and the control board. Battery Management System, performs functions that are vital in maintaining the safety and smooth functioning of the system such as cell protection, charge control, SOC determination, SOH determination, cell balancing, thermal management and communication. A network access panel is attached for receiving the outcome on a specific configured device.

Safety: The safety of the system is ensured through various parameters. The wheels contain tachometers. A cooling system is installed for the batteries to prevent hazardous fires. In case of emergencies, an e-kill switch is provided which cuts off power to the system. The actuation system of the braking mechanism is made such that in case of power failure, the brakes will engage bringing the pod to halt.
